Private Research Notes — L.N.
ii March

Confirmed: working at sub-Capitol locus (ref. my grid, vol. 4, p. 33) persists without maintenance. The Sigillum is Kincaid's — I would know his hand in wax or blood or stone — but something feeds it that isn't him. Either the seal was designed to feed itself, which he was not capable of in 2010, or it has been fed by someone who knows his methodology with an intimacy that disturbs me to contemplate.

Quaeritur: where is the anchor.

iii March

The seals do not ward. This took me a week to accept. They describe. There is a distinction here that Octavia would call pedantic and Octavia would be wrong. A ward is a door. A description is a map. I have been looking for a lock and I am standing in a cartographer's office.

Something has been mapped.

i April

The correspondence inverted along the vertical. Sicut inferius, sicut superius — as below, so above. Reversed deliberately. I have three centuries of Hermetic precedent in this room and not one of them inverts the vertical on purpose. You would have to decide to do this. You would have to decide it was correct.

The apparatus requires blood. Not as catalyst. Not as symbolic medium. As intake. I have found collection vessels at three nodes. They are not ritual objects. They are plumbing.

ii April

Found a sigil I cannot place. Not from any tradition I recognize and I recognize most. Sent rubbings to Heinrich, to Vasquez, to the woman in Lisbon whose name I am not writing down. None have written back. Heinrich always writes back.

I have drawn the sigil seven times in my notes trying to understand it. I have just noticed that the seventh iteration is larger than the others. I did not do that consciously.

iii April

It collects resonance. Not merely blood — the impressions held in blood. Lineage. Discipline. Memory of power. Every clan, metabolized and retained. I do not know where it is retained. I cannot find the vessel large enough to hold what it would need to hold by now.

Something is making a complete accounting of us. For what purpose a complete accounting.
